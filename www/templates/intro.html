Heredity is the process of passing traits from parents to offspring through genes.

The genetic traits you inherit from your parents are stored in complex threadlike structures called chromosomes.  You have 23 pairs of chromosomes in the nucleus of almost every cell in your body.

Each chromosome contains one molecule of DNA, which contains instructions affecting everything from your hair color to your sex. These instructions are broke into segments called genes.  You have about 30,000 or so genes; you inherit half of them from your mother and half from your father.
