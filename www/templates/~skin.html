SKIN COLOR REVEALS GEOGRAPHIC ANCESTORY   

Skin color is determined primarily by genes that produce melanin, a protein pigment in skin cells. Your skin color reflects the geographic origins of your ancestors. If you're dark-skinned, your ancestors most likely lived near the equator or in other areas with intense sunlight. If you're light-skinned, your ancestors probably came from more temperate regions of the globe. close
